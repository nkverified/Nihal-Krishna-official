About Maha Shivaratri

Maha Shivaratri, the "Great Night of Shiva," is one of the most significant festivals in Hinduism. It is celebrated in honor of Lord Shiva, the destroyer of evil and the transformer. Devotees observe fasting, perform night-long vigils, and offer prayers to seek blessings for health, wealth, and spiritual growth.

This sacred night is believed to be the day when Lord Shiva performed the Tandava, the cosmic dance of creation and destruction.

Significance of Maha Shivaratri

  • It marks the day Lord Shiva performed the Tandava, the cosmic dance of creation and destruction.
  • Devotees believe that observing Maha Shivaratri with sincerity washes away sins and liberates the soul.
  • It is also believed to be the night when Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati got married.
  • Offering Bilva leaves, milk, and honey to the Shiva Lingam is considered highly auspicious.
